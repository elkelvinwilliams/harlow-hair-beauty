Our Story

Loving people through
their transformation

Loving people through their transformation has always been part of who I am. It led me first into therapy — sitting with people as they grew, as they healed — before I understood that hair was offering me the very same gift, only in a language you could see.

The more I worked, the more it showed. Friends and family didn't simply leave looking different; they left feeling different — lighter, surer, more themselves. That became the vision: to bring both worlds beneath one roof, where the inner work and the outer finally meet.

Because a chair at Hagiazo is never just a chair. It's a place to exhale. To be cared for, unhurried. To feel that quiet confidence rise the moment you catch your reflection — and to know you were truly seen on the way there.

From the moment you arrive to the moment you leave, you begin the journey of becoming set apart. The transformation is never only physical; it is, as I've always believed, a renewing of the mind.

Close to fifteen years on, that vision is Hagiazo — box braids and knotless, cornrows, locs and retwists, bespoke wig-making and revamps, each met with the kind of care that renews.

The hands behind Hagiazo

Meet Ruvimbo

Ruvimbo is the founder, stylist and quiet force behind Hagiazo. Before the styling chair, there was the therapist's chair — years spent holding space for people as they grew and healed. Hair, she came to realise, did the very same work; only here, the transformation was something you could see.

Close to fifteen years on, she brings both gifts to every appointment: the precision of a master stylist, and the care of someone who truly sees you. From box braids and knotless to cornrows, locs and bespoke wigs, every style is unhurried and intentional — scalp-first, you-first — so you don't simply leave restyled. You leave set apart.
